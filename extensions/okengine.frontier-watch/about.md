# Frontier — capability-frontier whitespace

**What this is.** Each page here is a **whitespace-thesis**: a capability the market clearly
*wants* — discussed across many sources — but that few players actually *supply*. It's the open
edge of the capability frontier, surfaced from the vault's own demand/supply signal.

**Why it's useful.** Coverage lanes tell you what *exists*; this tells you what's *missing but
wanted* — research directions, product whitespace, positioning. It's built from the vault's real
graph (demand = source references, supply = entity references), not a model's hunch, and it
defers when the "gap" is just under-reported data rather than a real market gap.

**What you'll see on each page.**
- **capability** — the wanted-but-unsupplied capability
- **demand_signal / supply_state** — the evidence it's wanted, and how thinly it's provided
- **frontier_density** — the measured `demand N · supply M` signal (how warm the read is)
- **thesis** — the whitespace claim
- **lacuna_candidate** — set when a specific *force* keeping the cell empty is nameable (handed
  to [[lacuna/_about]] for the rigorous map→force→sort treatment, if that lane is enabled)

These are low-trust market inferences (`needs_review`), not verified facts — leads to test, not
verdicts.
