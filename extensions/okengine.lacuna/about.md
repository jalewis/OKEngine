# Lacuna — structural-gap discovery

**What this is.** Each page here is a *lacuna*: a cell the geometry of the existing knowledge
implies should exist, but which nothing occupies — paired with a **named force** that explains
why it stays empty. It is *not* a "we haven't written about X yet" coverage gap; it's a gap the
field's own structure points at.

**Why it's useful.** Asking a model for "a novel idea" regresses to the mean — the novel point
and the most-probable point pull in opposite directions. Lacuna instead runs a disciplined
6-step procedure that forces the output to a structural *edge* of the field, and grounds that
edge in **this vault's real concept→entity→source graph**, not a model's averaged recall. It
generates *coordinates* (research directions, whitespace, positioning) — not verdicts.

**What you'll see on each page.** The frontmatter mirrors the method 1:1:
- **field_mapped** — the region of the graph analyzed
- **hidden_axis** — what the whole field is secretly optimizing for
- **force** — the load-bearing reason the cell stays empty (no nameable force ⇒ the field is skipped)
- **discovered_vs_failed** — empty because *undiscovered*, or because *everything there fails*?
- **fill** — the proposed fill
- **confidence** + **surround_density** — the *measured* signal: thick fabric ⇒ stronger inference,
  thin patch ⇒ extrapolation

**Read these as low-trust.** Every page is `needs_review` and carries the caveat *"a structural
inference grounded in the cited subgraph, not a verified claim."* Where a fill is testable, it's
handed to predictions for grading — testing whether the floor holds is the operator's job.
